Daily Lesson Plan Final

School: San Felipe National High School Grade Level: 8 Quarter: 1
Teacher: Janice D. Quiñones Learning Area: Mathematics
Teaching Date and Time: April 5, 2026 | 8:00 AM - 9:00 AM

I. CURRICULUM CONTENT, STANDARDS AND LESSON COMPETENCIES

A. Content Standards
The learner demonstrates understanding of key concepts of fractions and decimals, including their relationship and conversions.
B. Performance Standards
The learner is able to formulate real-life problems involving fractions and decimals and solve these using appropriate strategies.
C. Learning Competencies and Objectives
  • Convert between fractions and decimals with denominators of 10, 100, and 1000. (M8NS-Ia-1)
  • Solve one-step word problems involving fractions and decimals using visual models.
  • Explain solutions orally using fraction and decimal language.
D. Content
Topic: Fractions and Decimals
Lesson: Converting Fractions to Decimals and Vice Versa
E. Integration
Inclusive Education Focus: This lesson integrates support strategies for learners with Difficulty in Reading (Dyslexia)
Accommodations:
  • Large, bold fonts (Arial/Comic Sans, 14pt min)
  • Oral responses instead of written answers
  • Preferential seating near the board
  • Extended time (1.5× standard time)
Modifications:
  • Reduced problems (10 to 5)
  • Simplified word problems
  • Focus on concept mastery over speed
  • Visual aids and manipulatives allowed
F. Observed Manifestations
No additional manifestations specified.
G. Additional Support Notes
No additional support notes specified.

II. LEARNING RESOURCES

  • Mathematics 8 Learner’s Module, DepEd
  • Fraction strips and decimal cards (printed)
  • Visual aids: Fraction-Decimal Conversion Chart
  • Worksheets with large fonts for SEN learners
  • Manipulatives: Base-ten blocks

III. TEACHING AND LEARNING PROCEDURE

A. Activating Prior Knowledge

Duration: 5 minutes

  • Review previous lesson on basic fractions using visual fraction strips.
  • Ask learners to identify fractions represented by shaded parts (oral responses accepted for SEN learners).
  • Use color-coded materials to help learners with dyslexia distinguish numerator from denominator.
B. Establishing Lesson Purpose

Duration: 5 minutes

  • Present real-life scenario: "How do we use fractions and decimals when shopping or measuring?"
  • Display learning objectives using large, clear fonts on visual aids.
  • Read objectives aloud for learners who benefit from auditory input.
C. Developing and Deepening Understanding

Duration: 25 minutes

Teacher’s Activity:

  • Model fraction-to-decimal conversion using think-aloud strategy.
  • Use step-by-step visual guides with icons (read, underline, compute, check).
  • Demonstrate using manipulatives alongside written examples.

Learner’s Activity (Differentiated):

Learner Group Activity Materials Time
SEN Learner Match fraction cards to decimal cards with peer support Large-print card set 15 min
Peer Group Solve fraction-decimal word problems in pairs Worksheet 15 min
Advanced Create own word problems and exchange with peers Notebook 15 min
Scaffolding Strategy: Pair the focus learner with a patient peer tutor trained to read directions aloud.
D. Making Generalization

Duration: 5 minutes

  • Ask learners: "How do we convert a fraction to a decimal?"
  • Allow oral responses; use visual summary chart for reference.
  • Summarize key points using color-coded fraction strips (red for numerator, blue for denominator).
E. Evaluating Learning

Duration: 10 minutes

Adaptive Assessment Methods:

  • Use oral recitation instead of written quiz for SEN learners.
  • Collect portfolio of completed fraction manipulative activities.
  • Observe participation and problem-solving strategies during group work.
  • 5-item practical assessment (reduced from 10 for SEN learners).
F. Teacher’s Remarks
G. Reflection
Referral Note: If reading difficulties persist across subjects despite accommodations, coordinate with the school’s SpEd focal person for further assessment and potential referral to allied professionals.
